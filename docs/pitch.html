It replaces paper registers, WhatsApp-group chaos, and manual spreadsheets with an integrated platform handling attendance, duty rosters, AI-powered timetables, parade management, multi-channel notifications, and AI-driven analytics.

2.2 The Scale of the Problem

  • 21,000+ secondary schools in Nigeria (115 Federal, 6,000+ state, 15,000+ private)
  • ~95% have no integrated digital operations system
  • 30+ hours/week lost to manual register-taking across the school
  • 2 weeks/term lost to manual timetable creation
  • Unfair duty loads — same staff repeatedly assigned, others never
  • Disconnected leadership — commandant can't see real-time attendance, duty completion, or task status

2.3 The Cost of Inaction

A typical Nigerian secondary school spends an estimated NGN 200,000+ annually on paper registers, printing, and filing supplies. The hidden cost of administrative time wasted on manual processes is 3-5x higher. AFCS eliminates both — a school saves its annual license fee in paper costs alone within the first term.

5.3 Why This Architecture Wins

  • Serverless — pay per request, not per server. Near-zero cost at low volumes. Scales to millions of requests without re-architecting.
  • Edge-rendered — pages load fast anywhere in Nigeria/Africa (Lagos, Kano, Port Harcourt all within Vercel's edge network).
  • Supabase — PostgreSQL with built-in auth, real-time subscriptions, and file storage. One service replaces five.
  • Telegram-first notifications — free, no Meta account required, no API costs. Works on basic phones and 2G networks.
  • Single codebase — one deployment serves all schools. Multi-tenant data isolation via PostgreSQL Row-Level Security.

11. Go-to-Market Strategy

Phase 1: Stitch in Time (Year 1) — 10 schools

Target: Federal Unity Colleges + private schools within 200km of Igbara-Oke

  • Direct sales — visit schools, demo on projector, offer free 30-day trial
  • Referral program — existing school gets 10% of Year 1 fees for referrals
  • AFCS command network — commandants across 6 zones are natural champions
  • Founder-led sales (months 1-6), then 1 sales rep (months 7-12)

Phase 2: Scale (Year 2-3) — 80 schools

Target: Private schools nationally, state government contracts

  • Multi-school product enables self-service onboarding
  • Partner with school associations (NAPS, ANCOPSS)
  • Digital marketing — Google Ads targeting "school management software Nigeria"
  • Attendance at education conferences (SEF, NAE)
  • Government pilots — offer 2 state governments free deployment for 6 months
  • 3 sales reps + 2 deployment engineers

Phase 3: Dominate (Year 4-5) — 200+ schools

Target: West Africa expansion

  • Franchise model — regional partners in Ghana, Benin, Cameroon
  • White-label option for large school groups
  • Education board contracts — provide BI dashboards for entire states
  • 8 sales reps across Nigeria + 3 regional partners
